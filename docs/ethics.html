Ethics

Ethical considerations were embedded at every stage of the project.

Ethics By Design

Urban Access was built with ethical AI practices as a first principle, shaping the problem definition, data collection, model training, and output design.

Community-Led Design

The routing focus, survey design, label categories, and output format were all shaped by consultation with Rashid Mushkani and ongoing feedback from Laurence Parent. The system reflects genuine disability needs and not assumptions made by the team on their behalf.

Label Bias Mitigation

The recalibration procedure is an active, methodological response to AI bias: it reduces the risk that AI-generated labels systematically misrepresent human accessibility judgements by anchoring them to human survey distributions before binarization.

Representation

Supporting six distinct mobility aid types ensures the system does not treat mobility impairment as monolithic. A manual wheelchair user and an electric wheelchair user have fundamentally different requirements, so the model captures this distinction explicitly.

Data Privacy

Survey data: fully anonymised. User accounts: secured in Supabase with row-level access controls. Account deletion: available any time. No movement data retained beyond what is needed for feedback weighting.

Honest Limitations

The VLM validation study found non-significant correlation between AI scores and human ground truth and we report this openly. The n=18 sample is underpowered, and more validation is needed. The MAE training was halted at 95/200 planned epochs. The walker class has lower F1 performance and known imbalance. These limitations are documented.

No Over-Reliance

The system provides route recommendations as suggestions with explained reasoning, not authoritative directives. Users retain agency and are expected to apply their own judgement alongside the system's output.

Privacy as a Design Principle

Ethical data practices were embedded from the outset. This applies to both the survey data used to calibrate our labels and the user data collected by the application.

🔒

Anonymised Survey Data

The 154 community ratings used to calibrate our AI-generated labels contain no personally identifiable informatio unless participants chose to optionally provide an email for follow up. Participants rated images only otherwise.

🗑️

User Data Control

App users can delete their account and all associated feedback data at any time from within the application, providing meaningful data agency.

🛡️

Secure Storage

User accounts and feedback are stored in Supabase with enterprise-grade security including row-level access controls. No movement data is retained beyond what is needed for feedback weighting.
